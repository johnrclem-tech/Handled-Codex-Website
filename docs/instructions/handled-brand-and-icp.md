## Summary

Handled is a fulfillment partner for eCommerce consumer brands that want a world-class branded delivery experience without building an internal operations team. The company combines bi-coastal warehousing, same-day operating guarantees, branded delivery execution, and transparent automation from checkout through returns.

Use this file as the source of truth for Handled's ICP, positioning, differentiation, proof points, services, and messaging rules when creating Google Ads, landing pages, SEO pages, website copy, or internal marketing tools.

## Target Market

Owners of eCommerce consumer brands in the United States that value a world-class delivery experience and want a partner to take care of it for them.

## ICP Definition

Best-fit customers:

- Owners or founders are the main contact.
- They do not have a dedicated head of operations.
- They sell from their own branded eCommerce store.
- Most sell on Shopify, but Handled supports all shopping carts including BigCommerce and WooCommerce.
- They may also sell through channels such as Amazon, Walmart, TikTok Shop, eBay, and Target.
- They sell consumer products where brand experience matters, especially fashion, apparel, cosmetics, personal care, and adjacent DTC categories.
- Their customers are in the United States.
- They are more attractive when they live near Handled warehouses or import through the Ports of NY/NJ or LA/Long Beach.

Poor-fit or lower-priority customers:

- Brands that only want the cheapest possible warehouse.
- Brands that do not value customer delivery experience.
- Brands with customers primarily outside the United States.
- Brands that require warehouse locations outside Southern California or New Jersey.
- Companies that already have a mature internal operations team and only need overflow storage.

## Positioning

Handled is the fulfillment partner for eCommerce brands that want the ideal customer delivery experience handled for them.

Core positioning posture:

- DTC first, with B2B supported when the ad group, page, or keyword intent calls for it.
- Brand experience first, not commodity logistics.
- Operational guarantee first, not vague "solutions" language.
- Conversational, clear, and owner-friendly.

## Three Uniques

- World-class branded delivery experiences.
- Transparency and visibility into everything.
- Fully automated from checkout to returns.

## Proven Process

The Handled Launch is a guided onboarding experience and operating system that engineers the ideal customer journey from checkout to return, fully automated with full transparency.

Draft process stages to refine:

- Discovery: understand product mix, order profile, packaging, channels, and customer expectations.
- Journey design: map the ideal delivery experience from checkout promise to return.
- Integration: connect shopping carts, marketplaces, inventory, orders, tracking, and returns.
- Launch: receive inventory, configure workflows, verify SLAs, and begin shipping.
- Optimization: monitor orders, restocks, support, packaging, and customer experience over time.

## Guarantee

The ideal customer delivery experience is the guarantee. Handled ships orders the same day with 100% accuracy, and when something goes wrong on Handled's end, Handled fixes it at its cost.

Homepage guarantee language:

- 100% Same-Day Shipping: orders placed by 12 PM ship out the same day, no exception.
- 100% Order Accuracy: if Handled makes a mistake, Handled covers the cost to fix it.
- 100% Same-Day Restocks: out-of-stock items are restocked the same day, without fail.

Use guarantees carefully:

- Use `100%` only for approved guarantees listed here.
- Avoid unsupported superlatives such as `best`, `#1`, or `guaranteed cheapest`.
- Do not create new guarantees without owner approval.

## Brand Name

Use `Handled`.

Do not use:

- `Handled3PL`
- `Handled.com`
- `Handled Inc.`

When `Handled` could read like a verb, solve ambiguity through sentence placement rather than changing the brand name.

## Segment Messaging Notes

Location-led:

- Emphasize proximity, port access, delivery speed, inventory placement, and regional warehouse fit.

Service-led:

- Emphasize operational capability, automation, SLA performance, and the customer journey from checkout to return.

Industry-led:

- Apparel and fashion: emphasize high SKU counts, size/color variants, returns, branded unboxing, seasonal spikes, and garment-safe handling.
- Cosmetics and personal care: emphasize brand experience, careful handling, batch/SKU complexity where relevant, and accuracy.

Integration-led:

- Emphasize automatic order sync, inventory visibility, tracking updates, and reduced manual operations.

Marketplace-led:

- Emphasize multi-channel operations, compliance where relevant, centralized inventory, and channel-specific fulfillment workflows.

## Google Ads Messaging Decisions

- CTA pool is universal-only.
- CTA character limit is 30 characters, hard ceiling.
- Description character limit is 90 characters, hard ceiling.
- Pinning strategy: partial pinning.
- No brand-named CTAs because of Google policy concerns.
- Do not use Shopify, Amazon, Walmart, or other platform names in CTA headlines.
- Use conversational language such as `our` and `a`.
- Avoid telegraphic ad-speak.
- Urgency timeline should be `2 weeks` or faster when relevant.
- `30 days` sounds too slow.
- Use `Handled` everywhere; solve verb ambiguity through placement rules.

## Claims To Avoid

- Unsupported superlatives such as `best`, `#1`, `cheapest`, or `fastest`.
- Claims that imply Google, Shopify, Amazon, Walmart, TikTok, or another platform endorses Handled.
- Unapproved guarantees beyond the guarantee section.
- Exact pricing or savings claims without approved support.
- Claims that Handled has live pages, locations, or capabilities not verified for the current website/project context.
